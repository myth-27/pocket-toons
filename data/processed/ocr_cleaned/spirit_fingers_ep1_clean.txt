=== Spirit Fingers Episode 1 ===

  (My name is Song Wooyeon. It's a perfectly ordinary name for a perfectly ordinary girl.)
  (My older brother, Song Geunwoo, is a genius law student, already mapping out his future with laser precision. My younger sister, Song Woojoo, is a talented dancer, graceful and confident, always in the spotlight.)
  (And me? I'm just... me. The middle child. The one who blends into the wallpaper. The invisible one.)
  (Every morning, it's the same routine. Wake up, get ready, try not to disturb anyone. Try not to make a sound.)
  (My mother's voice, always so clear, always so pointed.)
  Mother (thought) (thinking): "Wooyeon, try to be more like your brother. Or your sister." I just nod. What else can I do?
  (Today, it was another errand. A trip to the stationery store for Woojoo's special art supplies. Another task for the invisible girl.)
  [FOOTSTEPS (soft)]
  (As I walked through the park, my head down, trying to avoid eye contact with anyone, a strange sight caught my eye.)
  (A group of people. Not just any people. They were... colorful. Vibrant. Like a splash of paint on a muted canvas.)
  (One guy, in particular, stood out. Bright, fiery red hair that seemed to defy gravity. A loud, patterned shirt that screamed for attention. He was posing like a statue, one arm dramatically extended.)
  Wooyeon (thinking): What in the world are they doing?
  (Around him, others were sketching furiously. A girl with bright pink hair, meticulously shading her drawing. A guy with glasses and a beanie, his brow furrowed in concentration. Another, with a stern expression, but a surprisingly gentle hand on his sketchbook.)
  [SCRATCH (of pencils)]
  (They weren't just drawing; they were *living* in that moment. Laughing, chatting, completely absorbed in their world. So unbothered by the curious stares, the passersby.)
  (It was so different from my world, where every movement felt measured, every word carefully chosen to avoid drawing attention. To avoid being judged.)
  Wooyeon (thinking): And the red-haired guy... he had these incredibly long, slender fingers. Moving with such grace, even when he wasn't drawing. They almost seemed to... dance.
  Wooyeon (thinking): He looked so confident. So unselfconscious. So... unlike me.
  (I must have been staring. My heart pounded, a frantic drum against my ribs.)
  [THUMP-THUMP (heartbeat)]
  (Suddenly, the red-haired guy's eyes flickered in my direction. Just for a second. A quick, piercing glance that seemed to see right through me.)
  [GASPE (Wooyeon)]
  (I quickly looked away, my cheeks burning, clutching the bag of art supplies tighter. He didn't seem to notice. Or maybe he just didn't care.)
  [RUSTLE (bag)]
  (I hurried away, my cheeks still hot. It was embarrassing to be caught staring. To be caught at all.)
  (But even as I walked, I couldn't shake the image of them. The colors, the laughter, the sheer uninhibited joy. The way they just... existed.)
  Wooyeon (thinking): I clutched my bag, the weight of Woojoo's art supplies a strange irony. I was buying tools for someone else's creativity, while mine remained locked away, unexplored.
  Wooyeon (thinking): What would it be like to be so bold? To wear your personality on your sleeve, like that red-haired guy? To be part of something so vibrant, so unapologetically *them*?
  Wooyeon (thinking): My life felt like a grayscale photograph next to their vivid painting. A dull, muted existence.
  Wooyeon (thinking): The image of his 'spirit fingers' – those long, graceful digits – lingered in my mind. They seemed to hold a secret, a power I couldn't comprehend.
  (A tiny, almost imperceptible spark ignited within me. A flicker of something new, a quiet whisper that maybe, just maybe, I didn't have to be invisible forever.)
