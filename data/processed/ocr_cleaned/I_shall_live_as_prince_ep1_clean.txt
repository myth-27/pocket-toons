=== I Shall Live as a Prince Episode 1 ===

  (The world I knew was one of steel and glass, of information at my fingertips, of endless possibilities.)
  (Then, a blinding light, a crushing pain, and I woke up here. In a body that felt like it was constantly on the verge of collapse.)
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) Another day, another struggle just to breathe. This 'Prince Ciel' really drew the short straw, didn't he?
  [COUGH, COUGH]
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) My lungs feel like sandpaper. And this 'royal' bedchamber... it's practically a glorified broom closet compared to the main palace.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) The 7th Prince. Forgotten, frail, and utterly expendable. A perfect target for anyone looking to thin out the royal line.
  [RUSTLE of silk sheets]
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) I remember my old life. A successful entrepreneur, healthy, active. Now I can barely lift my head without feeling dizzy.
  [CREAK of a door opening]
  LIAN: Your Highness? Are you awake?
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) Lian. My only attendant. Loyal, but clearly resigned to my inevitable demise.
  PRINCE CIEL: Yes, Lian. I am.
  LIAN: Good morning, Your Highness. I've brought your morning herbal tea.
  [CLINK of ceramic on wood]
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) 'Herbal tea.' More like a concoction designed to keep me weak, or at least, not strong enough to cause trouble. I wonder what's actually in this stuff.
  PRINCE CIEL: Thank you, Lian.
  LIAN: The Head Physician sent word. He will be unable to visit today. He sends his apologies.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) Of course. The Head Physician. Too busy with the 'important' princes and princesses. My health is a low priority, if it's a priority at all.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) This isn't just neglect. This is calculated. Someone wants Prince Ciel out of the picture. And if I don't do something, 'I' will be out of the picture too.
  [DISTANT CHIMES from the main palace]
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) My modern mind, my knowledge... it's all I have left. I can't just lie here and wait to die. Not again.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) First, health. The body is a machine. It needs proper fuel, proper exercise, and proper rest. Not whatever this 'herbal tea' is supposed to be.
  PRINCE CIEL: Lian.
  LIAN: Yes, Your Highness?
  PRINCE CIEL: What are the common ingredients for these 'herbal teas' I've been given?
  LIAN: Oh, mostly common herbs, Your Highness. Ginseng, licorice root, ginger... sometimes a bit of wolfberry.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) Ginseng is good, but too much can be overstimulating for a weak heart. Licorice root can affect blood pressure. And the dosage... it's all about dosage and combination.
  PRINCE CIEL: And the food? What is typically served to me?
  LIAN: Light broths, Your Highness. Porridge. Soft vegetables. The kitchen staff are instructed to prepare meals suitable for your delicate constitution.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) 'Delicate constitution.' More like 'starved and malnourished.' This body needs protein, vitamins, minerals. Not just bland mush.
  PRINCE CIEL: Lian, from tomorrow, I want to try something different.
  LIAN: Different, Your Highness?
  PRINCE CIEL: Yes. I want to know what fresh fruits and vegetables are available in the market. And I want a small portion of lean meat with my meals. Something easily digestible, like chicken or fish.
  LIAN: But, Your Highness, the Head Physician's orders...
  PRINCE CIEL: The Head Physician isn't here, is he? And I am the Prince. My orders take precedence.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) A small step. But a crucial one. If I can't even control what goes into my own body, how can I control my destiny?
  LIAN: As you wish, Your Highness. I will inform the kitchen.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) Good. Now, what about this room? It's dusty, poorly ventilated. A perfect breeding ground for illness. Time for some basic hygiene and environmental control.
  PRINCE CIEL: Lian, after you've spoken to the kitchen, I need you to gather some cleaning supplies. And open these windows wide.
  LIAN: Open... the windows, Your Highness? But it's still quite cool outside.
  PRINCE CIEL: Fresh air is essential for health. And a thorough cleaning. Every corner. Under the bed, behind the curtains. No dust left untouched.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) They think I'm weak. They think I'm dying. Let them think that. While they underestimate me, I'll be rebuilding myself, piece by piece.
  PRINCE CIEL (thinking): (Thought) This isn't just about survival anymore. It's about living. And if I'm going to live, I'm going to live as a Prince who can stand on his own two feet.
  [SOUND of a window slowly creaking open, letting in a faint breeze]
