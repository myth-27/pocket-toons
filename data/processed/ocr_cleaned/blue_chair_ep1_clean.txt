=== Blue Chair Episode 1 ===

  (The first rays of morning sunlight, soft and hesitant, stretched across Mina's small apartment.)
  [ALARM BEEP (faint, then louder)]
  Mina: (Groans softly)
  Mina (thinking): Just five more minutes... No, ten. Ten glorious minutes.
  [THUMP (hand hitting snooze button)]
  (The familiar morning ritual. A dance between the desire for rest and the quiet insistence of a new day.)
  Mina (thinking): Ugh. The sun is already judging me.
  [STRETCH (long, satisfying)]
  (Eventually, the pull of caffeine proved stronger than the lure of the duvet. Mina shuffled into her tiny kitchen.)
  [WATER RUNNING]
  [COFFEE GRINDER WHIRR]
  Mina (thinking): The smell of fresh coffee... the only thing that makes mornings tolerable.
  [COFFEE DRIP (steady rhythm)]
  Mina (thinking): Another day, another blank canvas. Both literally and figuratively, I suppose.
  (Mina, a freelance illustrator, lived by her own rhythm. A rhythm often dictated by deadlines, inspiration, and the comforting presence of her workspace.)
  [CLINK (mug on counter)]
  (She carried her steaming mug to her desk, a corner of her living room dedicated to her craft.)
  (And there it was. Her anchor. Her confidante. The Blue Chair.)
  Mina: Good morning, old friend.
  [CREAK (softly, as she sits)]
  Mina (thinking): It's seen more of my triumphs and failures than anyone else. More coffee stains, too.
  (The chair, a slightly faded but still vibrant blue, molded perfectly to her form. It was a gift from her grandmother years ago, a piece of furniture that had somehow absorbed years of her dreams and anxieties.)
  Mina: Alright, Mina. Let's do this.
  (She powered on her drawing tablet. The screen glowed to life, displaying a half-finished illustration of a whimsical cityscape.)
  Mina (thinking): Still stuck on this rooftop garden. Needs... something.
  [CLICK (stylus picking up)]
  [PENCIL SCRATCH (digital, light)]
  Mina (thinking): Some days, the ideas flow like a river. Other days, it feels like trying to squeeze water from a stone. Today feels... like a gentle trickle.
  (She leaned back slightly, the blue chair supporting her, its familiar contours a comforting embrace.)
  Mina: (A soft sigh)
  Mina (thinking): Just gotta start somewhere. Even if it's just a single line, a tiny detail.
  (The world outside could be chaotic, demanding, or simply indifferent. But within the comforting embrace of her blue chair, Mina found her own quiet universe. A place where creativity could bloom, even on the most reluctant of mornings.)
  Mina: You always know how to make me feel better, don't you?
  (The blue chair, silent and steadfast, held her as she began to draw, one small stroke at a time.)
