=== Space Boy Episode 1 ===

  (They say home is where the heart is. But what if your heart is scattered across a thousand memories, all tied to a place you're about to leave forever?)
  AMY (thinking): ...
  AMY: Just one more box, Dad.
  FATHER: Almost ready, Amy? The shuttle leaves in an hour.
  [CRINKLE (of packing paper)]
  AMY: I know.
  AMY: It's just... hard to decide what's worth taking.
  FATHER: We've got plenty of storage on the ship. Don't stress too much about it. What matters is us.
  AMY (thinking): ...
  (He meant well. He always did. But how do you pack up a lifetime? How do you fit the smell of rain on pavement, the sound of my best friend's laugh, the way the sunlight hit my bedroom window in the mornings... into a box?)
  [CLICK (of a box lid closing)]
  AMY: Okay. I'm ready.
  FATHER: That's my girl. Come on, let's get you settled.
  (The shuttle ramp extended like a metallic tongue, swallowing us whole. The air inside was sterile, recycled. Nothing like the fresh breeze of Earth.)
  [HISS (of the ramp closing)]
  [HUM (a low, constant thrum of the ship)]
  AMY: It's... quiet.
  FATHER: Most of the crew are already in cryo-sleep. It's a long journey, sweetie.
  AMY: How long, again?
  FATHER: Three years. Give or take a few months, depending on the stellar currents.
  AMY: Three years.
  AMY (thinking): (Thought) Three years of nothing but this hum. Three years away from everything I've ever known. And then, a new planet. A new school. A new everything.
  (I walked through the narrow corridors, past rows of sealed cryo-pods. Each one a silent sleeper, dreaming of a destination I couldn't even picture.)
  [BEEP (a soft, rhythmic pulse from a console)]
  AMY: Is this our cabin?
  FATHER: Yep. Compact, but it's home for the next few years. And look!
  FATHER: Your very own viewport.
  (He gestured to a small, circular window. I peered out, expecting to see stars, or perhaps the receding blue marble of Earth.)
  AMY: It's... black.
  AMY (thinking): (Thought) Just an endless, inky blackness. No stars, no planets, no familiar light. Just the void.
  FATHER: We haven't left the atmosphere yet, sweetie. Give it time.
  (But even as he spoke, I felt it. A subtle lurch, a deep, resonant rumble that vibrated through the floor, through my very bones.)
  [RUMBLE (deep, powerful engine ignition)]
  FATHER: There we go. Liftoff.
  AMY (thinking): ...
  (The black outside the viewport began to swirl, then streak. Not with light, but with the blurring speed of our ascent.)
  (I pressed my hand against the cool glass, watching as Earth, my home, became nothing more than a memory, receding into the vast, indifferent expanse.)
  AMY (thinking): (Thought) Goodbye, Earth. Hello... nothing.
