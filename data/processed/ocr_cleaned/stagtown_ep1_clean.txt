=== Stagtown Episode 1 ===

  (The map had called it 'Stagtown.' A dot on the periphery, a place you only passed through if you were truly lost, or truly seeking something forgotten.)
  (I was the latter, I suppose. Or maybe just foolish.)
  Maeve (thinking): Alright, Maeve. Deep breaths. It's just a town. A very... quiet town.
  [CRUNCH (gravel under tires)]
  [RUMBLE (engine dies)]
  Maeve: Huh. Guess this is it.
  [CREAK (car door)]
  (The air was thick here, heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else... something metallic, like old blood.)
  (The buildings were hunched, their windows like vacant eyes staring out from under heavy brows of moss and grime. No cars. No people. Just the wind.)
  [WHISTLE (wind)]
  Maeve: Hello? Anyone home?
  Maeve (thinking): My voice just... vanished. Swallowed by the silence.
  (A faded sign above a dilapidated storefront read: 'GENERAL GOODS & SUNDRIES.' The door was ajar, a sliver of darkness beckoning.)
  [CREAK (door slowly opens)]
  Maeve: Hello?
  [CLINK (something falls inside)]
  Maeve (thinking): Okay, definitely not empty.
  Old Man: Lost, are we?
  (He emerged from the shadows at the back, a gaunt figure with eyes that seemed too old, too knowing.)
  Maeve: No, not lost. Just... passing through. Looking for a place to stay the night.
  Old Man: Passing through Stagtown? Few do. Fewer still stay.
  Maeve: Is there a motel? An inn?
  Old Man: The Old Lodge. Up the hill. But it's not for everyone.
  Maeve: What's that supposed to mean?
  Old Man: Means the town has its ways. Its... expectations.
  Maeve (thinking): He’s giving me the creeps. Just get what you need and go.
  Maeve: Do you have any bottled water? And maybe a map of the area?
  Old Man: Water, yes. Maps... not much use here. Roads change. Memories fade.
  [THUMP (something heavy outside)]
  Maeve: What was that?
  Old Man: Just the town breathing. It does that.
  (He handed me a dusty bottle. As I paid, my gaze drifted to a carving on the counter. A stylized deer head. Antlers like twisted branches.)
  Maeve: That's... interesting. A local symbol?
  Old Man: It's the Stag. Always watching. Always waiting.
  Maeve (thinking): His eyes. They weren't looking at me. They were looking *through* me. Like I was already part of the scenery.
  Maeve: Thanks. I'll... find the Lodge.
  [CREAK (door closes)]
  (Back on the street, the silence felt heavier, more deliberate. The wind whispered, and for a moment, I could almost hear a faint, distant call. A guttural sound, like something ancient stirring.)
  [LOW MOAN (distant, unsettling)]
  Maeve (thinking): No. Just the wind. It has to be.
  (But the feeling of being watched intensified. Every shadow seemed to deepen, every window a pair of unseen eyes. Stagtown wasn't just quiet. It was waiting.)
