=== Killer Peter Episode 1 ===

  (The city hummed, a distant, indifferent beast stirring from its slumber. But in Apartment 4B, a different kind of silence reigned. A meticulous, almost surgical stillness.)
  PETER (thinking): Another day. Another cycle. The illusion of purpose, meticulously maintained. The gears turn, the cogs mesh. A precise mechanism.
  [TICK-TOCK (faint, precise, from a minimalist wall clock)]
  PETER (thinking): Three minutes to brew. One minute to cool. Sixty seconds to consume. Efficiency. Predictability. The bedrock of a stable existence. Any deviation is a flaw. A weakness.
  [CLINK (spoon against ceramic)]
  PETER (thinking): The world outside is a chaotic symphony of noise and impulse. A constant, irritating static. Here, in these walls, I control the frequency. I dictate the rhythm.
  PETER (thinking): They rush. To what? To be seen? To be heard? To be... remembered? A futile endeavor. Most are just background noise. Disposable. Their lives, a series of predictable errors.
  (The bus was a metal cocoon, filled with the scent of stale coffee and unfulfilled dreams. Peter sat by the window, a silent observer in a world that rarely observed back.)
  PETER (thinking): The young woman with her headphones, lost in a manufactured reality. The businessman, eyes glued to a screen, chasing phantom profits. The old man, clutching his newspaper, clinging to fading headlines. Each a character in their own mundane tragedy. So easily distracted. So easily... guided.
  [RUMBLE (bus engine)]
  PETER (thinking): I see the tells. The nervous twitch, the averted gaze, the slight tremor in their hands. They carry their fears like badges. And their secrets, like heavy stones.
  (Peter walked the hallowed halls of the city's central library, a temple of knowledge, or perhaps, a mausoleum of forgotten thoughts. His domain.)
  [FOOTSTEPS (soft, deliberate, echoing slightly on polished floors)]
  PETER (thinking): The scent of old paper, of dust, of forgotten stories. A comforting shroud. Unlike the stench of ambition, of fear, of desperation that clings to the living. The raw, unfiltered scent of humanity.
  SARAH: Morning, Peter! Early bird as always. Got a new shipment of historical texts in. Exciting stuff! Anything particularly gruesome this time? You know how you like the darker histories.
  PETER: Morning, Sarah. Indeed. History has a way of repeating itself, doesn't it? The patterns are always there, for those willing to look beyond the superficial.
  SARAH: You always have such a profound way of putting things. Makes me feel like I'm missing something. Anyway, I'll be at the front desk. Shout if you need anything. Don't get lost in the archives!
  PETER (thinking): Shout? No. I prefer silence. And control. And I am never lost. Only searching.
  PETER (thinking): The irony is not lost on me. Reading about the desperate acts of lesser men. Their clumsy attempts at art. Their inevitable failures. So much wasted potential. So much... mess.
  PETER (thinking): Amateurs. No finesse. Too much emotion. The thrill wasn't in the act, but in the *planning*. The *perfection*. The absolute, pristine execution.
  [THUMP (a heavy book drops nearby, startlingly loud in the quiet)]
  MR. HENDERSON: Oh, dear! My apologies, Peter. Clumsy old fool. My hands aren't what they used to be.
  PETER: No trouble, Mr. Henderson. Accidents happen. Though, one must always be careful. The unexpected can be... disruptive.
  PETER (thinking): Accidents. Or opportunities. The line is often blurred. A moment of weakness, a lapse in attention. All it takes.
  PETER (thinking): Weak. Fragile. Like most of them. So easily broken. And so many hidden marks. Each a story. Each a vulnerability.
  PETER (thinking): Imperfection. It gnaws at me. The loose thread, the misplaced word, the lingering trace. It must be erased. Every detail accounted for. Every surface pristine.
  PETER (thinking): Each a story. Each a potential variable. And some, a solution. The city is a puzzle. And I, its quiet solver.
  [WHISPERS (faint, distant, like the library itself is breathing, a low, constant hum)]
  (The silence in the library was not empty. It was pregnant with unspoken thoughts, with hidden desires, with the quiet hum of a predator observing its environment. Peter, the quiet observer, was just beginning his day. And for some, their day was about to end. The first move on the board had been made.)
  [CREAK (a floorboard under Peter's foot, almost imperceptible, but chillingly present in the silence)]
