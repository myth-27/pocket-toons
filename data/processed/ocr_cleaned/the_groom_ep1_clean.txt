=== The Groom Episode 1 ===

  (The city lights blurred into a watercolor painting outside Lee Hana's window. Another night, another shift, another empty plate on the table.)
  Lee Hana (thinking): Ugh... My feet are killing me.
  (Her small apartment, barely more than a single room, offered little comfort. The air was thick with the smell of instant noodles and damp, old wallpaper.)
  [DRIP... DRIP...]
  Lee Hana (thinking): Still raining, huh? Just like my life.
  (Hana slumped onto her worn mattress, the weight of her grandmother's medical bills pressing down on her more than the fatigue. She closed her eyes, wishing for a miracle, or at least a moment of peace.)
  [THUMP]
  Lee Hana (thinking): What was that?
  (A heavy thud against her door startled her. It wasn't the usual hurried knock of the landlord. This felt... deliberate.)
  [CREAK]
  Lee Hana: Hello? Is anyone there?
  (Silence. Only the relentless drumming of rain against the windowpane answered her. Cautiously, Hana approached the door, peering through the peephole. Nothing. Just the dim, flickering hallway light.)
  [RUSTLE]
  (Then she noticed it. A single, ornate envelope lying on her doormat. It wasn't standard mail. The paper was thick, almost like vellum, and sealed with a dark, unfamiliar wax stamp.)
  Lee Hana (thinking): What is this? I didn't order anything...
  (Her fingers trembled slightly as she picked it up. The wax seal depicted a stylized, thorny rose. A strange chill ran down her spine.)
  [CRACKLE]
  (She broke the seal, the paper feeling oddly cold beneath her touch. Inside, a single card, black as midnight, with silver calligraphy.)
  Lee Hana: An invitation... to a wedding?
  (Her eyes scanned the elegant script.)
  Lee Hana: 'You are cordially invited to celebrate the union of... Lee Hana... and... The Groom.'
  Lee Hana (thinking): What? My name? And 'The Groom'? Who is this? This has to be a prank.
  (She flipped the card over, searching for a sender, an explanation. Instead, a single line of text, stark and unsettling, gleamed back at her.)
  Lee Hana: 'Your presence is not merely requested, but required. On the eve of the Blood Moon, at the Manor of Whispers. Refusal is not an option.'
  [GASP]
  Lee Hana (thinking): Blood Moon? Manor of Whispers? What kind of sick joke is this?
  (A sudden, inexplicable chill permeated the room, raising goosebumps on her arms. The air grew heavy, like a presence had entered. She clutched the card, its elegant beauty now twisted into something menacing.)
  Lee Hana (thinking): This isn't funny. I don't know anyone named 'The Groom'. And I certainly didn't agree to marry anyone!
  (As if in response, a faint, almost imperceptible whisper seemed to drift from the card itself, a sound like dry leaves rustling in a forgotten tomb.)
  [WHISPER... (faint, guttural)]
  Lee Hana (thinking): No... no, no, no. I'm just tired. Overworked. My imagination.
  (But the whisper lingered, a promise of something ancient and terrible. And etched into the silver calligraphy, almost invisible until now, was a single, additional word beneath 'The Groom': 'Yours.')
  Lee Hana (thinking): Mine?
  [THUMP-THUMP-THUMP (heartbeat)]
  (The rain outside intensified, mirroring the frantic pounding of her heart. Hana was no longer just tired; she was terrified. Her life, already a struggle, had just taken a turn into an unknown nightmare.)
