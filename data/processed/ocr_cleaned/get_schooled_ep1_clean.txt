=== Get Schooled Episode 1 ===

  (Daewon High. A name that once signified academic excellence, now whispered with a different kind of reputation. A place where the curriculum included daily lessons in fear, and the teachers often looked the other way.)
  (It was a school in crisis. And I was here to fix it.)
  Na Hwajin (thinking): Another one.
  [CLATTER]
  Na Hwajin (thinking): The broken window on the third floor. The graffiti in the stairwell. The hushed whispers of students avoiding eye contact. It's all so painfully familiar.
  [MURMUR]
  Na Hwajin (thinking): They're not even trying to hide it anymore.
  Kim Min-jun: You got it, Jin-woo? My 'lunch money'?
  Lee Jin-woo: I... I told you, I don't have any more. My parents cut off my allowance.
  Park Sang-ho: Oh, really? So you're saying you're just going to starve Min-jun hyung, huh? That's cold, man. Real cold.
  [THUMP (a fist hitting the wall next to Jin-woo's head)]
  Lee Jin-woo: Ngh!
  Kim Min-jun: Look, I'm a reasonable guy. But if you can't pay, then maybe you can 'work it off'. There's always the school store, right? Or maybe you can 'borrow' some stuff from the teachers' lounge.
  Na Hwajin: Excuse me.
  [FOOTSTEPS (calm, deliberate)]
  Kim Min-jun: Who the hell are you?
  Na Hwajin: I'm Na Hwajin. I just transferred here. And I couldn't help but overhear.
  Park Sang-ho: Overhear what, punk? This is none of your business. Go back to your old school.
  Na Hwajin: It becomes my business when it happens in my line of sight. 'Lunch money', you said? And 'working it off'?
  Kim Min-jun: Yeah, so what? We're just having a friendly chat.
  Na Hwajin: Friendly chats usually don't involve threats of property damage or coercion. And they rarely involve one party looking like they're about to wet themselves.
  Lee Jin-woo: (Whispering) Please, just go...
  Park Sang-ho: You think you're tough, new kid? You don't know who you're messing with.
  Na Hwajin: Oh, I think I have a pretty good idea. Kim Min-jun, right? Son of Assemblyman Kim Byung-ho. And Park Sang-ho, your father owns the local construction company.
  [FREEZE (bullies)]
  Kim Min-jun: How... how do you know that?
  Na Hwajin: It's my job to know. Or rather, it was. Now, about this 'lunch money'. If I were to report this, not to the school, but directly to the Ministry of Education's Special Anti-Bullying Task Force, how do you think your fathers would react?
  Park Sang-ho: The... the Task Force?
  Na Hwajin: Yes. The one that's been quietly investigating schools with high rates of unaddressed violence and corruption. The one that has the power to shut down institutions and ruin reputations.
  Na Hwajin: And I'm sure they'd be very interested in how the sons of prominent figures are contributing to the problem.
  Kim Min-jun: You're bluffing. You're just a student.
  Na Hwajin: Am I? Or am I someone who knows exactly how to make a formal complaint stick, how to gather evidence, and how to ensure it reaches the right desks? Someone who knows the difference between a 'friendly chat' and criminal extortion.
  Na Hwajin: I suggest you return Lee Jin-woo's property and find another way to fund your lunches. Or perhaps, just eat the school cafeteria food like everyone else.
  [GRIT (Kim Min-jun's teeth)]
  Kim Min-jun: This isn't over, new kid.
  Na Hwajin: Oh, I assure you, it's just beginning.
  [RUSTLE (bullies retreating hastily)]
  Lee Jin-woo: Th-thank you...
  Na Hwajin: Don't thank me. Thank yourself for surviving this long. But next time, don't let it get this far.
  Na Hwajin (thinking): Daewon High. It's going to be a long semester. But I've dealt with worse. Much, much worse.
