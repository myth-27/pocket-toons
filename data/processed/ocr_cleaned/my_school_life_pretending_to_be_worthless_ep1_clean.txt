=== My School Life Pretending to Be a Worthless Person Episode 1 ===

  (The fluorescent lights of Class 2-3 hummed, a monotonous drone that perfectly matched the rhythm of my 'worthless' existence.)
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) Another day. Another performance. It’s exhausting, but necessary.
  LEE JIN-HO: Look who it is. Our resident punching bag, Kim Min-woo.
  PARK SUNG-MIN: Still got that pathetic look on your face, Min-woo? It's almost lunchtime, you know. Someone's gotta pay for our snacks.
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) Here we go. Cue the 'helpless victim' act.
  KIM MIN-WOO: Ah... Jin-ho... Sung-min... I... I don't have much today.
  CHOI HYUN-WOO: Don't have much? What, did your allowance get cut because you're such a loser?
  [SHOVE!]
  KIM MIN-WOO: Ugh!
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) That push was weak. They're getting complacent. Good.
  LEE JIN-HO: Empty your pockets, Min-woo. Unless you want to spend your lunch break in the infirmary.
  KIM MIN-WOO: Okay... okay... here.
  [CRUMBLE]
  (I pulled out a crumpled 5,000 won bill and a few coins, offering it with a trembling hand. The perfect picture of a terrified student.)
  PARK SUNG-MIN: Is this all? Seriously? You're even worthless at earning money.
  [GRAB!]
  LEE JIN-HO: Whatever. It's better than nothing. Let's go, guys. This loser isn't worth our time.
  [FOOTSTEPS FADE]
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) They always leave just enough for me to buy a cheap bread. They're not completely evil, just typical high school thugs. Ignorant of the beast they're poking.
  (I watched them leave, my face a mask of relief. But inside, a different emotion simmered. Not anger, but a cold, calculated patience.)
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) It's been three months since I enrolled here. Three months of playing the part of Kim Min-woo, the weakest link. The 'worthless person.'
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) They think they're strong. They think they're untouchable. They have no idea what true strength looks like.
  [CLENCH]
  (My hand, which had just trembled, now clenched into a fist, the knuckles turning white. The force was enough to crack the desk if I allowed it.)
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) The goal is simple: survive this school year unnoticed. No fights, no heroics, no revealing my true capabilities. Just blend in, graduate, and disappear.
  KIM MIN-WOO (thinking): (thought) But even a 'worthless person' has limits. And I can feel them being tested, day by day.
  [BELL RING!]
  (The lunch bell rang, pulling me back to the present. Time to go buy that cheap bread. And keep the act going.)
