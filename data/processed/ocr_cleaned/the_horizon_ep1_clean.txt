=== The Horizon Episode 1 ===

  (The world had ended, not with a bang, but with a whimper. And then, a scream. A thousand screams, echoing across a landscape scarred by human madness.)
  Boy (thinking): Another day. Another empty stomach. The sun, a cold eye in a sky that refused to cry.
  [CRUNCH (of dry leaves underfoot)]
  (He moved like a shadow, a ghost among the ruins. Every rustle, every distant shout, a potential executioner. His name, a forgotten whisper. His purpose, to simply exist.)
  Boy (thinking): The others… they’re always looking. For food. For anything. For a reason to take.
  [DISTANT SHOUTS. A THUD.]
  (He pressed himself against the crumbling wall of what was once a supermarket, the smell of decay thick in the air. The sounds were getting closer. The hunters.)
  Boy (thinking): Don’t move. Don’t breathe. Just… disappear.
  [SCRAPE (of a heavy boot on concrete)]
  MAN 1: See anything, you old dog?
  MAN 2: Just dust, boss. And the ghosts of what used to be.
  MAN 1: Ghosts don’t fill bellies. Keep looking.
  Boy (thinking): They’ll pass. They always do. Eventually.
  [FOOTSTEPS RECEDING SLIGHTLY.]
  (He waited, heart hammering against his ribs, a frantic bird trapped in a cage of bone. Then, a different sound. A soft whimper. Not animal. Human.)
  Boy (thinking): No. Not again. Don’t look. Don’t get involved. That’s how you die.
  [WHIMPER (faint, desperate)]
  (The sound pulled at him, a fragile thread in a world of broken ropes. Against his better judgment, he peered around the corner of the collapsed aisle.)
  (A girl. Smaller than him, maybe six or seven. Curled into a ball, clutching a tattered teddy bear, her face streaked with dirt and tears. A fresh cut bled sluggishly on her arm. She was hiding behind a stack of rusted cans, barely concealed.)
  Boy (thinking): She’s alone. Like me. But… she’s hurt. And they’re still close.
  [DISTANT LAUGHTER (from the men)]
  Boy (thinking): If they find her…
  (He knew what happened to children found alone. He’d seen it. He’d run from it.)
  Boy (thinking): Run. Just run. She’s not your problem.
  Girl: M-Mommy…
  Boy (thinking): Mommy isn’t coming. Nobody is.
  (His legs felt heavy, rooted to the spot. The whimpers grew more frequent, more desperate, threatening to draw attention.)
  Boy (thinking): She’s going to get us both killed.
  (He took a breath, the stench of dust and despair filling his lungs. A decision, made in the space between heartbeats.)
  Boy: Hey.
  Girl: !
  (Her eyes, wide and terrified, met his. They were the color of a stormy sky, full of rain that wouldn’t fall.)
  Boy: Don’t make a sound. Come with me. Now.
  (He didn’t wait for an answer. He reached out, his hand hesitant, then firm, grasping her small, trembling wrist.)
  Girl: Where…?
  Boy: Away.
  [RUSTLE (as they move quickly but quietly)]
  (He pulled her, gently but urgently, through the debris, away from the fading laughter of the men. They moved through the skeletal remains of the city, a silent pact forged in fear.)
  Boy (thinking): What have I done? I was alone. That was safe. Now…
  (He glanced back at her. She stumbled, but kept pace, her grip on the teddy bear unwavering. Her eyes, still wide, now held a flicker of something else. Not hope, not yet. But perhaps… a question.)
  Boy (thinking): The horizon. That’s where we’re going. Even if I don’t know what’s there. It has to be better than this.
  (And so, two children, strangers bound by a shared terror, began to walk. Towards nothing. Towards everything. Towards the horizon.)
