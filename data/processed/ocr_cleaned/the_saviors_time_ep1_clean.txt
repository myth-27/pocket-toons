=== The Savior's Time Episode 1 ===

  (The metallic tang of blood filled my mouth, the screech of tires still echoing in my ears. Then, nothing. Just the familiar, suffocating darkness.)
  [GASP!]
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): My eyes snapped open. The fluorescent lights of the classroom hummed above me. The scent of dust and old textbooks filled my nostrils. This again.
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): My heart hammered against my ribs, a frantic drumbeat against the silence of the early morning classroom. The clock on the wall read 7:58 AM. Two minutes before the bell.
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): Another rewind. Another failure. This time, it was the crosswalk. Minjun, distracted, stepping out right as the delivery truck rounded the corner. A blur of red, then…
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): I clenched my fists, nails digging into my palms. How many times? How many deaths would it take before I could save him?
  (I pushed myself up from my desk, my movements stiff. My gaze immediately swept across the room, searching. He wasn't here yet.)
  [FOOTSTEPS (distant)]
  Kang Minjun: Hey, Jinwoo! You're early today.
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): Minjun. His smile, bright and carefree, was a dagger to my chest. He had no idea. No idea of the loops, the deaths, the burden.
  Lee Jinwoo: Just… trying to get a head start on homework.
  Kang Minjun: Seriously? You're such a goodie-two-shoes. Come on, let's grab some coffee from the vending machine before class starts. My treat.
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): No! Not the vending machine. That’s where… no, that was a different loop. But still, the hallway is always a risk.
  Lee Jinwoo: Uh, actually, I forgot my wallet. I'll just… stay here.
  Kang Minjun: Don't be silly, I said my treat! Come on, it'll only take a minute. I saw some new flavors.
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): Minjun grabbed my arm, pulling me towards the door. My heart pounded. I had to keep him safe, but how? Every time I tried to change something, another variable emerged.
  (We walked down the bustling hallway. Students laughed, chatted, oblivious. I scanned every face, every corner, every loose tile. The crosswalk, the rooftop, the chemistry lab… all places he'd died before.)
  (Minjun was already at the vending machine, excitedly pointing at a new drink. I stood a few feet back, my eyes darting around.)
  [CREAK (from above)]
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): My head snapped up. A maintenance panel in the ceiling directly above Minjun was loose. It swayed precariously.
  Lee Jinwoo: Minjun! Get back!
  Kang Minjun: Huh? What are you—
  [The panel ripped free, plummeting towards Minjun. Time seemed to slow. I lunged forward, shoving him with all my might.]
  Kang Minjun: Oof! Jinwoo, what the hell?!
  (The panel smashed onto the floor where Minjun had been standing a split second before, scattering dust and debris. A collective gasp rippled through the hallway.)
  Lee Jinwoo: Are you okay?
  Kang Minjun: Yeah, I think so… What was that?
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): My hands were shaking. I'd saved him, just barely. But this wasn't how he died last time. This wasn't the truck. It was always something new, something unexpected. The killer wasn't a person, or an event. It was… fate itself.
  (My gaze fell on a small, intricately carved wooden charm lying amidst the debris near the fallen panel. It wasn't there before. A chill ran down my spine.)
  Lee Jinwoo (thinking): No, I'm not giving up. I'll find out what's doing this. I have to.
