=== My Dear Cold-Blooded King Episode 1 ===

  (The sun hadn't even kissed the peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains, and already, the day's toil weighed heavy. My hands, calloused from years of chopping firewood and kneading dough, moved with practiced ease.)
  KIHARA (thinking): This was my life. Not the grand, romantic tales spun by bards, but the gritty reality of a commoner in the Kingdom of Haim.
  KIHARA (thinking): They say our King is a benevolent ruler, but benevolence doesn't fill empty stomachs or mend worn-out shoes. And then there's his son, Prince Rihan. The 'Cold-Blooded King.' A name whispered in hushed tones, often accompanied by a shiver. A formidable warrior, they say, but one whose heart is said to be as frozen as the northern wastes.
  KIHARA (thinking): I try not to dwell on such lofty matters. My kingdom is here, within the confines of our small home, amidst the scent of freshly baked bread and the warmth of our hearth. Royal affairs are a distant thunder, a storm meant for others. Not for me.
  (The market, a vibrant tapestry of life, was just beginning to hum. Kihara, with a basket of her family's renowned apple tarts, navigated the growing crowds.)
  MASTER ELARA: Morning, Kihara! Your tarts smell heavenly today!
  KIHARA: Good morning, Master Elara. Trying to outdo your spiced nuts, are we?
  MASTER ELARA: A friendly competition, always. Heard the Prince is passing through today. Hope he doesn't scare off all my customers.
  KIHARA (thinking): Prince Rihan. Of course. It's always something when he's around.
  (The sudden blare of trumpets pierced the market's din, followed by the stern commands of the Royal Guards. A wave of silence, heavy and immediate, swept through the crowd, replacing the lively chatter with anxious whispers.)
  [TRUMPETS BLARE!]
  GUARD CAPTAIN: Clear the path! Make way for His Royal Highness, Prince Rihan!
  KIHARA (thinking): Here we go. Brace yourself, Kihara.
  (At the forefront of a formidable retinue, a lone figure on a magnificent black steed commanded all attention. Clad in dark, austere armor, his presence was a chilling force. Prince Rihan. His face, etched with a perpetual solemnity, was framed by raven hair, his eyes like polished obsidian, devoid of warmth.)
  [HOOVES THUDDING]
  KIHARA (thinking): The rumors are true. He truly is a king of ice. Even from this distance, I can feel the oppressive weight of his aura.
  (As the Prince's horse slowed to a measured pace, his piercing gaze swept over the bowing populace. It was a look that seemed to dissect, to find every weakness. For a fleeting, terrifying moment, those dark eyes locked onto Kihara, who, still clutching her basket, had only managed a shallow bow, her curiosity momentarily overriding her fear.)
  KIHARA (thinking): Don't look away. Don't show fear. You're just a girl selling tarts. He has no reason to care. But why does it feel like he's looking *through* me?
  (Just as his gaze began to move on, a sudden jostle from a frantic passerby sent Kihara stumbling. Her basket, brimming with apple tarts, flew from her grasp, scattering its fragrant contents across the dusty cobblestones.)
  [CRASH!]
  KIHARA: Oh, no!
  (The small, embarrassing commotion brought the Prince's procession to a halt. His dark eyes, which had just averted, now returned to Kihara, kneeling amidst her ruined wares. There was no anger, no pity, just... an unnerving, unblinking observation.)
  KIHARA (thinking): This is it. I'm going to be reprimanded. Or worse.
  RIHAN: Guard.
  GUARD: Yes, Your Highness?
  RIHAN: Ensure the path is clear. We have wasted enough time.
  (His words, though not directed at her, felt like a direct dismissal. With a subtle flick of his wrist, Rihan signaled his guards, and the procession resumed its stately march, leaving Kihara on the ground, the scent of cinnamon and apple mingling with the dust.)
  KIHARA (thinking): He didn't even acknowledge me. Not even a glance of disdain. Just... nothing. As if I were less than the dirt beneath his horse's hooves.
  (Slowly, Kihara began to gather the few salvageable tarts, her hands trembling slightly. The market, still hushed, began to stir back to life, but the oppressive atmosphere lingered.)
  KIHARA: The 'Cold-Blooded King' indeed. But... for a moment, when our eyes met, it felt like... he saw something. Something beyond just a clumsy commoner.
  (As she stood, brushing dust from her apron, Kihara couldn't shake the image of his unreadable eyes. A cold, unsettling encounter, yet one that had ignited a strange, unwelcome flicker of curiosity within her. Her world, once so clearly defined, had just brushed against a force that threatened to unravel it.)
