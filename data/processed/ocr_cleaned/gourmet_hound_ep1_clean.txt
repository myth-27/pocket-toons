=== Gourmet Hound Episode 1 ===

  (The world, for most, is a symphony of senses. For Lucy, it's a cacophony.)
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) Plain rice, plain chicken breast. Again.
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) It's the only way. Anything else... it's too much. Too loud.
  [CLINK (fork against ceramic)]
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) The metallic tang of the fork, the faint chemical scent from the plate's glaze, the residual oil from the last wash... it all screams.
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) And the food itself? A ghost. A whisper of what it should be, drowned out by everything else.
  (Lucy's hyperosmia wasn't a gift. It was a burden, turning every meal into a chore, every restaurant into a sensory assault.)
  (She worked at 'The Daily Grind,' a cozy neighborhood cafe, a place ironically filled with the very smells that tormented her.)
  [HISS (espresso machine)]
  [CLATTER (dishes)]
  [CHIMES (door opening)]
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) The bitter roast of coffee, the cloying sweetness of pastries, the faint, lingering scent of last night's spilled milk... it's all a single, overwhelming cloud.
  LUCY: (To herself, murmuring) Order up! Latte for table four!
  (She moved through the cafe, wiping tables, clearing plates, her senses constantly assaulted.)
  [SCRAPE (chair moving)]
  [WHIRR (blender)]
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) Another half-eaten croissant. The buttery scent, usually so appealing to others, is just... fat. Heavy. Greasy.
  (Then, as she reached for a plate left behind by a hurried customer...)
  [WHOOSH (a subtle, distinct current of air)]
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) Wait.
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) What is that?
  (Amongst the usual chaos of smells, a single, clear note cut through. It was subtle, almost gone, clinging to a forgotten crumb on the plate.)
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) It's... clean. Warm. A hint of something earthy, something sweet, but not cloying. Like sunshine after rain.
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) It's not just a smell. It's a memory. A feeling.
  [SNIFF (Lucy takes a deep, almost desperate breath)]
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) It's... perfect. The first perfect thing I've smelled in years. Maybe ever.
  (Her eyes darted around the cafe, searching for the source. The customer was long gone. Only a few crumbs remained on the ceramic.)
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) What was it? A scone? No, too complex. A pastry? Maybe, but not like any I've tasted here.
  [THUMP (Lucy gently sets the plate down, careful not to disturb the scent)]
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) This isn't just a smell. It's... a promise. A taste I need to find. A taste that could finally cut through the noise.
  (For the first time in a long time, Lucy felt a flicker of hope. A purpose beyond simply enduring.)
  LUCY (thinking): (Thought) I have to find it. Whatever it was. Whoever made it.
  [SWISH (Lucy picks up the plate, a determined look on her face)]
  (Her quiet, bland world had just been shaken by a single, perfect scent. And her hunt had just begun.)
