=== In the Bleak Midwinter Episode 1 ===

  (The wind howled a ceaseless dirge, a symphony of frost and desolation. It had been like this for as long as anyone could remember.)
  (The 'Great Chill,' they called it. A planetary catastrophe that turned Earth into a tomb of ice, leaving behind only the skeletal remains of a forgotten world.)
  [CRUNCH. CRUNCH. CRUNCH.]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) Another day. Another hundred clicks across the ice-sheet. My 'lucky' day, if the scanner isn't lying.
  (Kael, a silhouette against the perpetual twilight, trudged onward. His environmental suit, a patchwork of scavenged tech, hissed softly with every step.)
  [WHIRRRR (low hum from suit)]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) The power cells are almost depleted. One more cycle, maybe two, before I'm just another frozen statue in this wasteland.
  [BEEP. BEEP. BEEP. (scanner alert)]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) Finally. A heat signature. Faint, but it's there. Deep within Sector 7-Gamma. The old 'Arcology-Prime' ruins.
  (He adjusted his visor, the HUD flickering to display a faint red thermal outline beneath layers of snow and collapsed ferrocrete.)
  [GUST (wind whips)]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) Arcology-Prime. The last bastion of warmth, they said. Before the 'Fall.' Now, just another freezer.
  (He reached the entrance, a gaping maw of twisted metal and shattered duraglass, partially buried under a glacial drift.)
  [CREEEAAAK (metal groaning)]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) Standard procedure. Check for structural integrity. Look for traps. And pray the 'cold ones' haven't made this their den.
  [WHOOSH (air escaping as Kael enters)]
  (Inside, the air was still, thick with the dust of centuries. Frozen debris hung suspended, caught in the last moments of collapse.)
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) The scanner's getting stronger. Whatever it is, it's deep inside. And it's radiating a lot of residual energy.
  [CLINK (small piece of ice falls)]
  (He navigated through the labyrinthine corridors, his headlamp cutting through the gloom, illuminating murals of a vibrant, green Earth.)
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) Lies. All lies. This world was always meant to freeze.
  [BEEP-BEEP-BEEP-BEEP! (scanner intensifies)]
  (He pushed open a heavy, frozen door, revealing a vast, circular chamber. In the center, encased in a block of ice, pulsed a faint blue light.)
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) A cryo-pod? No, too small. And the energy signature... it's not human.
  (As he approached, the ice around the object began to crack, a delicate web spreading across its surface.)
  [CRACKLE. SHATTER. (ice breaking)]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) What in the...?
  (The ice fell away, revealing a sleek, metallic device, intricate and alien. It hummed with an internal power, the blue light growing brighter.)
  [HUMMMMMMM (device activating)]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) This isn't just a power cell. This is... something else entirely. Something from before the Chill. Something powerful.
  (Suddenly, a low growl echoed from the shadows, a sound Kael knew all too well.)
  [GRRRRRRRRRR (deep, guttural)]
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) Damn it. The cold ones. They must have sensed the energy spike.
  (Two glowing red eyes emerged from the darkness, followed by a hulking, frost-covered silhouette. A 'Chill-Beast.')
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) No time to analyze. Just run. Or fight. And I'm out of ammo.
  [ROOOOAAARRR! (Chill-Beast)]
  (Kael gripped the strange device, its blue light pulsing in his hand, a beacon in the encroaching darkness.)
  KAEL (thinking): (Thought) This changes things. Everything changes now.
