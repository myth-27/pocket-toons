=== Is It Fortune or Is It Woe Episode 1 ===

  (They say everyone has a guardian angel. Mine must be on permanent vacation, sipping margaritas in some celestial resort, completely oblivious to the chaos that is my life.)
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) Another Monday. Another chance for the universe to play its cruelest jokes on Joo Hwa-yeon. First, my alarm didn't go off. Then, my toast decided to commit arson. And don't even get me started on the coffee.
  Joo Hwa-yeon: Just breathe, Hwa-yeon. Today is different. Today is the day. The interview for 'Starlight Media'! My dream job. Nothing can go wrong.
  [RUMBLE (distant thunder)]
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) Oh, come on! It was perfectly sunny five minutes ago!
  [SPLASH! (a puddle of water)]
  Joo Hwa-yeon: AAH!
  (A rogue puddle, a speeding taxi, and my pristine white blouse. A classic Hwa-yeon trifecta.)
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) No, no, no! This can't be happening! Not today!
  [THUD! (a heavy object falling)]
  Joo Hwa-yeon: My portfolio!
  (As I scrambled to pick up the scattered papers, a shadow fell over me. A tall, impeccably dressed shadow.)
  Kang Do-jin: Having a rough morning?
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) That voice! Smooth as silk, but laced with… amusement? I looked up, and my breath hitched. He was impossibly handsome. Like he’d walked straight out of a webtoon. And he was holding my most important document, completely unharmed.
  Joo Hwa-yeon: Uh, yes. You could say that. Thank you.
  Kang Do-jin: You dropped this. It seems important.
  (He handed me the document, his fingers brushing mine. A strange jolt, like static electricity, shot through me. My heart did a weird flutter-skip thing.)
  Joo Hwa-yeon: It… it is. It's for an interview.
  Kang Do-jin: Starlight Media, I presume?
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) How did he know?! He must have seen the logo. Still, it felt… unsettlingly precise.
  Joo Hwa-yeon: Yes. How did you…?
  Kang Do-jin: I'm heading that way myself. Perhaps our paths are aligned.
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) Aligned? Or catastrophically intertwined? My bad luck radar was blaring. This man was too perfect, too calm, too… coincidental.
  [DRIP, DRIP (rain starting)]
  Joo Hwa-yeon: Oh, great. Now it's raining.
  Kang Do-jin: Here.
  (He opened a sleek, black umbrella, shielding both of us from the sudden downpour. For a moment, I felt a strange sense of calm. A tiny, fleeting bubble of good fortune.)
  Joo Hwa-yeon: Thank you.
  Kang Do-jin: No need. We're going the same way.
  (We walked in silence for a few blocks, the rain pattering around us. His presence was… overwhelming. I couldn't shake the feeling that something significant had just happened. Or was about to.)
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) He saved my portfolio, sheltered me from the rain… is this what good luck feels like? After years of dating disasters, job rejections, and general cosmic mischief, could this be a turning point? Or is he just a really handsome harbinger of even worse woe?
  [HONK! (a car horn blares loudly)]
  Joo Hwa-yeon: Whoa!
  (A delivery scooter swerved dangerously close, nearly clipping the umbrella and sending a fresh wave of dirty water onto my already ruined blouse.)
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) Nope. Still me. Still cursed. And now I’m walking under an umbrella with a stranger who probably thinks I’m a walking disaster zone. Is he my fortune, or is he just another chapter in my book of woe?
  Kang Do-jin: Careful. Some people are in too much of a hurry.
  (He glanced at me, a faint, almost imperceptible smile playing on his lips. And for the first time, I noticed the small, intricate silver charm hanging from his wrist. It looked like a tiny, stylized four-leaf clover.)
  Joo Hwa-yeon (thinking): (Thought) A four-leaf clover? Is he mocking me? Or is it… a sign? This day just got a whole lot more complicated.
