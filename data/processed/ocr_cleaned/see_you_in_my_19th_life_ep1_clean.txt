=== See You in My 19th Life Episode 1 ===

  (Some say life is a journey. For me, it's a cycle. A never-ending loop of beginnings and endings. I've lived... 18 lives.)
  (Each time, the memories stack. The joy, the sorrow, the love, the regrets. All of it, perfectly preserved, like an antique collection in my mind.)
  (And then, there was the 18th. The life that changed everything.)
  YOUNG SEO-HA: Noona, are you really leaving?
  JOO-WON: Just for a little while, Seo-ha. I'll be back before you know it. We'll go to the amusement park, remember?
  YOUNG SEO-HA: Promise?
  JOO-WON: Promise. Now, be a good boy for your father.
  (That day, the rain was relentless. And so was fate.)
  [TIRES SCREECHING]
  [CRASH]
  [GLASS SHATTERING]
  JOO-WON (thinking): Seo-ha... I'm sorry. I broke my promise.
  (And just like that, Yoon Joo-won ceased to exist.)
  [BABY CRYING]
  (The familiar darkness, then the blinding light. The overwhelming sensation of a new body, small and helpless.)
  (A new name. Ban Ji-eum. A new family. A new life. But the old memories remained, sharp and clear.)
  JI-EUM (thinking): No... not again. I just... I just died.
  MOTHER: Oh, my precious Ji-eum. You're finally here.
  JI-EUM (thinking): Seo-ha... Seo-ha! I have to find him. I *have* to.
  (From the moment I could walk, I was different. My 'childish' drawings were architectural blueprints. My 'playtime' was spent devouring encyclopedias. And my 'imaginary friends' were often figures from my previous lives, offering unsolicited advice.)
  YOUNG JI-EUM: No, no, the optimal strategy for this stock market fluctuation is clearly a short-sell on the tech sector, not a long-term hold. Honestly, these analysts from 1998 had no foresight.
  MOTHER: Ji-eum-ah, why aren't you playing with the other children? They're discussing the new cartoon.
  YOUNG JI-EUM: Eomma, I'm researching corporate mergers. And besides, I already know how that cartoon ends. It's a rehash of a 17th-century French folktale, poorly adapted.
  MOTHER: Oh, my unique little girl... (sighs fondly) You're going to be someone very special.
  JI-EUM (thinking): Special, yes. But not for fame or fortune. Only for him.
  (Every decision, every study session, every skill I acquired, was a calculated step towards one person. Moon Seo-ha.)
  (I learned to play the piano because he loved music. I learned business because his family ran a powerful conglomerate. I even learned to cook his favorite dishes, just in case.)
  JI-EUM: Alright, Ban Ji-eum. You're 25. You've built yourself from the ground up. You're fluent in four languages, a certified accountant, and have a black belt in taekwondo from your 14th life. You're ready.
  JI-EUM (thinking): I know he's still alive. I felt it. But finding him... after all these years, after the trauma... will he even remember Joo-won? Will he be okay?
  JI-EUM: It doesn't matter. I promised. And this time, I won't break it. I'll make him happy.
  [DOOR OPENS]
  [FOOTSTEPS ECHOING]
  (My target: MI Group. His family's conglomerate. My new job application: accepted. My mission: reunite with Moon Seo-ha. And maybe, just maybe, make him smile again.)
  (The 19th life. This time, it's not about letting go. It's about holding on. And finding my way back to him, no matter what.)
