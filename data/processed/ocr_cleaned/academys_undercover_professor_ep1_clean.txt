=== Academy's Undercover Professor Episode 1 ===

  (The Grand Academy of Eldoria. A bastion of magic, knowledge, and… surprisingly bland tea.)
  IAN (thinking): (Thought) To think, I, the 'Shadow Weaver,' would find myself in such a place. My new mission: 'Professor Ian.' What a farce.
  IAN: Good morning, students. I am Professor Ian. I will be your instructor for 'Basic Mana Theory and Application' this semester.
  STUDENT A: Professor Ian? Never heard of him.
  STUDENT B: He looks… rather ordinary.
  LADY SERAPHINA: Professor, with all due respect, our previous instructor, Professor Eldrin, was renowned. What qualifications do you possess to teach such a fundamental course?
  IAN (thinking): (Thought) Ah, the typical noble-born arrogance. Always eager to test the new blood. Good. It keeps things interesting.
  IAN: My qualifications are sufficient, Lady Seraphina. However, I believe true understanding comes not from titles, but from application. Let's begin with a simple exercise.
  IAN: Focus your mana. Feel its flow, its inherent resistance. Now, attempt to conjure a basic light orb. Don't worry about perfection, just the attempt.
  [FWOOSH. ZZZZT. POP.]
  LADY SERAPHINA (thinking): (Thought) This is child's play. He's testing us with kindergarten spells.
  LADY SERAPHINA: Mine is complete, Professor.
  (A brilliant, fist-sized orb of pure light floated above her palm, radiating power.)
  IAN: Excellent, Lady Seraphina. Now, hold it. Feel the mana draining, the instability. Tell me, how long can you maintain that perfect form?
  LADY SERAPHINA: As long as I wish, Professor. My control is—
  [SHIMMER. CRACKLE.]
  IAN (thinking): (Thought) Her mana control is indeed exceptional for her age. But she lacks the fundamental understanding of mana's inherent entropy.
  IAN: Observe.
  (Ian extended a single finger. No grand gesture, no visible spell circle. Yet, the air around Lady Seraphina's orb subtly shifted.)
  [WHOOSH. FIZZLE.]
  LADY SERAPHINA: W-what? My orb… it dissipated!
  IAN: It simply returned to its natural state. You focused on creation, but neglected the principles of dissolution and stability. A powerful spell is not just about its initial burst, but its longevity and control.
  IAN (thinking): (Thought) A simple mana disruption wave, disguised as a gentle breeze. They won't suspect a thing.
  PROFESSOR THORNE: Professor Ian, a moment of your time?
  (Professor Thorne, a portly man with an air of self-importance, approached Ian after class.)
  IAN: Professor Thorne. How can I assist you?
  PROFESSOR THORNE: Just wanted to offer a word of advice. The students here, especially the noble houses, expect a certain... gravitas. Your methods seem a touch unorthodox for a foundational course.
  IAN: I believe in practical application, Professor. Theory alone often falls short.
  PROFESSOR THORNE: Indeed. But one must maintain decorum. Ah, careful there!
  [CREAK. THUD.]
  (A stack of ancient, heavy tomes on a nearby shelf began to tilt precariously, threatening to crash down.)
  PROFESSOR THORNE: My word! That's the rare 'Compendium of Forbidden Runes'! It'll be ruined!
  IAN (thinking): (Thought) Clumsy. A simple stabilization charm would prevent this. But then, Thorne's 'gravitas' wouldn't allow him to be seen casting such a basic spell.
  [WHOOSH. CLINK.]
  (Just as the books began their final descent, a faint, almost imperceptible shimmer of air passed beneath them. They settled back onto the shelf with a soft, almost inaudible 'clink'.)
  PROFESSOR THORNE: Oh! How… fortunate. It seems the shelf merely shifted. Good thing it didn't fall. That would have been quite the mess for a new professor to clean up.
  IAN: Indeed. Perhaps the academy should consider reinforcing its older fixtures.
  IAN (thinking): (Thought) 'Fortunate,' he calls it. The irony. This academy is a nest of vipers, and I'm supposed to be the unassuming bird. Let's see how long this charade can last before the real game begins.
