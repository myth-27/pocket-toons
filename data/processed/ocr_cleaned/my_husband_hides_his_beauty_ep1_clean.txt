=== My Husband Hides His Beauty Episode 1 ===

  (The Marquisate of Valois was once a name whispered with reverence, a beacon of prosperity. Now, it was a name synonymous with ruin.)
  LETICIA (thinking): Another dawn. Another day closer to my fate. My father's debts... my family's last hope. All resting on my shoulders, a sacrificial lamb offered to the beast of the North.
  LETICIA (thinking): They call him the 'Beast of the North,' Duke Erden. A man so disfigured, so cruel, that even the bravest knights refuse to serve in his court. Whispers say he wears a mask not for dignity, but to spare others the horror of his visage. A monster, they say. My husband.
  LETICIA (thinking): I've steeled myself for this. I expect nothing but a cold, lonely existence. Perhaps that's for the best. No grand illusions to be shattered, no expectations to be disappointed. If he truly is a monster, then I will simply exist, a shadow in his shadow, and find my own quiet peace.
  (The journey to the Duchy of Erden was long, arduous, and filled with a silence heavier than the leaden skies above. The Valois carriage, once grand, now rattled along a desolate path, carrying its most precious, and most desperate, cargo.)
  LETICIA (thinking): The landscape grows more desolate with every mile. No flowers, no vibrant greens. Only stark, skeletal trees and an endless, grey horizon. It mirrors the emptiness in my chest.
  MAID: My Lady, are you not afraid? They say the Duke... he has eyes that burn like embers, and a voice that chills to the bone. No one has seen his true face and lived to tell the tale without nightmares.
  LETICIA (thinking): Afraid? Of course, I am. But what good would fear do now? I've already accepted my lot. All I ask for is a quiet corner, a small measure of peace, even in this 'monster's' keep.
  [CARRIAGE WHEELS GRIND TO A HALT]
  [HEAVY CASTLE GATES GROAN OPEN]
  (Erden Castle loomed like a jagged scar against the twilight sky. Its stone walls, dark and ancient, seemed to absorb all light and warmth, casting long, menacing shadows.)
  LETICIA (thinking): It's even more formidable than the rumors described. A fortress, not a home. This is where I am to spend the rest of my days.
  HEAD BUTLER: Welcome, Lady Leticia. The Duke awaits you.
  LETICIA (thinking): His voice is as cold as the stone around us. No warmth, no welcome. Just a duty to be performed.
  (She was led through echoing halls, each step a drumbeat against the oppressive silence, until she reached a vast, dimly lit study. The air was heavy, thick with an unspoken tension.)
  [HEAVY OAK DOORS CREAK OPEN]
  LETICIA (thinking): There he is.
  (Seated at a massive desk, shrouded in shadow, was a figure draped in dark velvet. A heavy, ornate mask, intricately carved with swirling, almost predatory patterns, obscured his entire face. Only two narrow slits for eyes, from which no light seemed to escape, were visible.)
  LETICIA (thinking): The mask... it was not a simple covering. It was a work of art, crafted from dark metal. It covered his entire face. It was designed to intimidate, to conceal, to terrify. And it succeeded.
  DUKE ERDEN: You are late.
  LETICIA (thinking): His voice. Deep. Resonant. Not a growl, but a low rumble that vibrated through the floorboards, through my very bones. It wasn't harsh or guttural, as I'd imagined. It was... controlled. Powerful. Commanding.
  LETICIA: My apologies, Your Grace. The journey was... extensive.
  DUKE ERDEN: Indeed. You may take your leave. The arrangements for your stay have been made. My steward will brief you on your duties.
  LETICIA (thinking): Duties? Not 'our' duties, not 'as my wife'. Just 'your' duties. He truly sees me as nothing more than a transaction. A burden.
  LETICIA: As you wish, Your Grace.
  (As she curtsied, preparing to retreat, a sudden draft swept through the room, making the heavy tapestries billow. For a fleeting instant, the candlelight flickered, and a shadow shifted across the Duke's form.)
  LETICIA (thinking): Just beneath the edge of his mask, where his jawline should have been, I caught a glimpse. A sliver of skin. Not scarred, not grotesque, but... smooth. Impossibly pale. It was gone before I could even register it, swallowed by the returning shadows. My heart gave a strange jolt. A trick of the light, surely. My mind playing games.
  [THUMP-THUMP (Leticia's heart)]
  (But the shadows quickly reclaimed him, and the monstrous mask was all that remained. Leticia exited the room, her mind reeling, a tiny seed of doubt planted amidst the fear.)
