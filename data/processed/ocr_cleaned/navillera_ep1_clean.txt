=== Navillera Episode 1 ===

  (Seventy years. A lifetime, they say. But what does a lifetime truly mean?)
  (For seventy years, Shim Deok-chul lived. He worked, he married, he raised children. He did what was expected.)
  (But sometimes, in the quiet moments, a forgotten dream would stir. A whisper from a past self.)
  [WIND CHIMES JINGLE]
  Deok-chul (thinking): (thought) The wind is getting colder. Autumn is truly here.
  [RUSTLE (of leaves)]
  (One afternoon, while walking through the park, a sight caught his eye. A young man, barely out of his teens, was dancing.)
  [SWISH (of fabric)]
  [TAP-TAP (of ballet shoes on pavement)]
  Deok-chul (thinking): (thought) Ballet... So light. So free. Like a bird taking flight.
  (He watched, mesmerized. The world around him faded, leaving only the dancer's elegant movements.)
  Deok-chul (thinking): (thought) I remember... when I was a boy. I saw a performance on a small black and white TV. I wanted to try. Just once.
  (But life had other plans. Work. Family. Responsibilities. The dream was tucked away, forgotten under layers of practicality.)
  [DOOR CREAKS OPEN]
  Hae-nam: Deok-chul? You're home late. Dinner's getting cold.
  Deok-chul: Ah, Hae-nam. Sorry. I was just... walking.
  Hae-nam: Walking? You've been doing a lot of 'walking' lately. Is something on your mind? You're not getting sick, are you?
  Deok-chul: No, no. Just enjoying the autumn air.
  Deok-chul (thinking): (thought) How could I tell her? My wife, who has spent her life worrying about my health, my pension, our children. She'd think I've lost my mind.
  Hae-nam: Well, come eat. The children called. Our daughter, Eun-ho, got a promotion. And our son, Seong-san, is thinking of investing in a new business.
  Deok-chul: That's good news. Our children are doing well.
  Hae-nam: Yes. They're all grown up now. We've done our part. Now it's time for us to rest.
  Deok-chul (thinking): (thought) Rest. Is that all there is left? Just... rest?
  [CRICKETS CHIRP]
  (Later that night, unable to sleep, Deok-chul found himself staring at the ceiling. The dancer's image replayed in his mind.)
  Deok-chul (thinking): (thought) Seventy. Too old, they'd say. Too frail. What's the point?
  Deok-chul (thinking): (thought) But... what if? What if there's still time for just one more thing? One thing, just for me?
  [RUSTLE (of newspaper)]
  (The next morning, with a trembling hand, he picked up the community newspaper. His eyes scanned the classifieds.)
  Deok-chul: (reading aloud softly) 'Ballet Studio... beginner classes available...'
  (A single phone number. A small, almost imperceptible tremor of hope.)
  [DIAL TONE]
  Deok-chul: (clears throat) Hello? Is this... the Simok Ballet Studio?
