=== Maybe Meant to Be Episode 1 ===

  (Another Sunday. Another family dinner. And another interrogation.)
  Jia's Mother: Jia-ya, look at your cousin, Sunmi. She's already planning her second child. And you? Still single at twenty-nine!
  Jia's Aunt: It's not that she's not pretty, Jeong-ah. She just needs to lower her standards a little. Or maybe try harder?
  Han Jia: Eommoni, Ajumma. I’m doing perfectly fine. My career is thriving. My new project just got approved.
  Jia's Mother: Career, career, career! What's a career if you're going to grow old alone? Who will take care of you?
  NARRATOR (thinking): Alone. That word always stings. Not because I'm afraid of it, but because they make it sound like a disease.
  NARRATOR (thinking): My company just promoted me to team lead. I'm finally seeing the fruits of my labor. Why can't they see that's enough?
  [SIGH]
  Han Jia: I'm not 'alone.' I have friends, colleagues, a full life. Marriage isn't the only path to happiness.
  Jia's Mother: Oh, for heaven's sake, Jia! Just go on a few blind dates. Meet someone nice. You're not getting any younger!
  NARRATOR (thinking): Blind dates. The endless parade of awkward conversations, forced smiles, and men who just want a trophy wife. No, thank you.
  NARRATOR (thinking): But... what if there was another way? A way to shut them all up, to buy myself some peace and quiet, without actually sacrificing my independence?
  NARRATOR (thinking): The thought, absurd and desperate, flickered through my mind: A contract marriage.
  NARRATOR (thinking): It was a ridiculous idea, something out of a drama. Who would even agree to such a thing?
  NARRATOR (thinking): Then, a face popped into my head. Someone equally plagued by family expectations. Someone I'd known since we were kids.
  NARRATOR (thinking): Lee Mincheol. The quiet, reliable son of the Lee Group. Also twenty-nine. Also single. Also, probably, just as fed up as I was.
  NARRATOR (thinking): He was always so calm, so logical. The perfect business partner, even for something as outlandish as this.
  [RING! RING!]
  Han Jia: Oh, excuse me. I need to take this.
  (I grabbed my phone, eager for an escape. The caller ID made my breath hitch.)
  NARRATOR (thinking): Lee Mincheol. Speak of the devil.
  Han Jia: Hello?
  Lee Mincheol: Jia. It's Mincheol. Do you have a moment to talk? There's something important I need to discuss with you.
  (His voice was as calm and steady as ever, but there was an unusual edge to it. A hint of urgency.)
  NARRATOR (thinking): Could it be? Was he thinking what I was thinking?
  Han Jia: Mincheol? What's up? Is everything okay?
  Lee Mincheol: I'm fine. But... I have a proposition for you. A rather unconventional one.
  NARRATOR (thinking): My heart started pounding. This was either going to be the most insane conversation of my life, or the most brilliant.
  NARRATOR (thinking): Maybe... just maybe... we were meant to be partners in crime.
