=== Down to Earth Episode 1 ===

  (Kade's world was a carefully curated ecosystem of code, caffeine, and the comforting hum of his PC. His apartment, a sanctuary from the unpredictable chaos of the outside, rarely saw uninvited guests.)
  KADE (thinking): Just a few more lines of code... then I can finally fix that rendering bug. This game isn't going to finish itself. Another all-nighter, probably. But hey, at least I don't have to deal with... people.
  [CLICK-CLACK (keyboard)]
  KADE (thinking): The silence is nice. Peaceful. Just me and my thoughts. And maybe a few thousand lines of C++.
  [WHOOSH (a soft, distant sound)]
  KADE: Huh?
  KADE (thinking): Was that... a plane? No, too close. Too... soft. Like something falling, but not crashing.
  [THUD. (A gentle, muffled impact from the backyard)]
  KADE: What in the world...?
  KADE (thinking): Probably just a branch. Or maybe a raccoon got into the trash again. Better check. Last time, it was a whole family of them. Cute, but destructive.
  [CREAK (door opening)]
  [CRUNCH (grass underfoot)]
  (The cool night air hit Kade's face, a stark contrast to the stale warmth of his apartment. He squinted into the darkness of his small backyard, the faint glow from his neighbor's porch light barely illuminating anything.)
  KADE (thinking): Nothing. See? Just my imagination. Or maybe I'm finally losing it from lack of sleep.
  [RUSTLE (from behind the large oak tree)]
  KADE: Hello? Is anyone there?
  KADE (thinking): Okay, definitely not a raccoon. Too big for that. And it's not moving like an animal.
  (Kade cautiously stepped closer, his heart beginning to thump an irregular rhythm against his ribs. He peered around the thick trunk of the ancient oak.)
  (And there she was. Not a raccoon. Not a branch. Definitely not a plane.)
  (A girl. Or something that looked like a girl.)
  KADE: Whoa!
  KADE (thinking): What in the actual...? She's... glowing? And her hair... it's like a nebula. And what is she wearing? Is that... a spacesuit? No, it's more like a shimmering, form-fitting fabric.
  ZAIDA: Greetings, inhabitant of Sector 7-G.
  KADE:  (stumbling back) Sector... what? Who are you? How did you get in my yard?
  ZAIDA: My vessel experienced an unscheduled atmospheric re-entry. My apologies for the disturbance to your... foliage.
  KADE (thinking): Vessel? Foliage? Is this some kind of prank? A very elaborate, very expensive prank. But her eyes... they're so wide. And... purple?
  ZAIDA: My designation is Unit 734, but you may refer to me as Zaida. I am here to observe.
  KADE: Observe...? Observe what? Me?
  ZAIDA: Primarily. Your species, specifically. Your planet's unique atmospheric composition and diverse biological structures are of great interest to the Intergalactic Federation.
  KADE (thinking): Intergalactic Federation? Okay, I'm definitely dreaming. Or I've finally snapped. Maybe I should just go back inside and pretend this never happened.
  ZAIDA: You appear distressed. Is this a typical human reaction to unexpected encounters with non-native life forms?
  KADE:  (swallowing hard) Uh, yeah. Pretty much. Especially when they fall out of the sky and talk about intergalactic federations.
  ZAIDA: My apologies. My landing sequence was... less than optimal. My energy core requires immediate replenishment. Do you possess any high-density caloric paste? Or perhaps a suitable energy conduit?
  KADE (thinking): Caloric paste? Energy conduit? She's asking for food, right? Or... a charger?
  KADE: I have... instant ramen? And a power strip?
  ZAIDA:  (tilting her head, a hint of curiosity in her voice) Instant ramen. Is that a primary sustenance source for your species?
  KADE:  (sighing) For me, sometimes. Look, Zaida, I don't know what's going on, but you can't just... be here. My neighbors...
  ZAIDA: My presence must remain undisclosed. My mission parameters dictate minimal interference. However, my current state of depletion compromises my ability to maintain stealth.
  KADE (thinking): Stealth? She just crashed in my backyard!
  ZAIDA: Therefore, I require temporary shelter and assistance with energy restoration. Your dwelling appears... adequate.
  KADE: My dwelling? You want to stay in my house? With me?
  ZAIDA: Affirmative. It would facilitate optimal observation and minimize external contamination.
  KADE (thinking): This is insane. Absolutely, undeniably insane. But... she looks so lost. And kind of... beautiful, in a completely otherworldly way. What am I even thinking?
  ZAIDA: Your internal monologue indicates a conflict between logical assessment and emotional response. A fascinating human trait.
  KADE:  (blushing) You can hear my thoughts?!
  ZAIDA: A rudimentary telepathic link is established for efficient communication. It facilitates data acquisition.
  KADE (thinking): Oh god. This is going to be a long night.
  KADE:  (rubbing his temples) Look, Zaida. Let's just... go inside. We need to talk. And maybe I have some... ramen.
