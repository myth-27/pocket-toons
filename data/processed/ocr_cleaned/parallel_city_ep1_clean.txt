=== Parallel City Episode 1 ===

  (Aethelburg, Cycle 237. Another dawn, another cycle.)
  (The city hums, a symphony of progress and precision. Every citizen, every cog, perfectly placed.)
  SYSTEM VOICE: Good morning, Citizen Ren. Cycle 237, 06:00. Optimal wake-up achieved. Hydration protocol initiated.
  Ren (thinking): (Yawn) Optimal, huh? Feels pretty standard to me.
  [HISS (of a water dispenser)]
  Ren (thinking): Another day of processing data streams for the Central Archive. Thrilling.
  (Ren downs the nutrient paste, the same taste, the same texture, every single morning. Aethelburg thrives on consistency.)
  [CLICK-WHIRR (of apartment door sealing)]
  (The sky-lanes are already bustling. Hover-pods glide silently, ferrying citizens to their designated sectors.)
  Ren (thinking): Look at them. All plugged in. All content. Sometimes I wonder what they dream about.
  [WHOOSH (of a passing hover-pod)]
  (Ren boards the Sector 7 Monorail. The familiar drone, the familiar faces, all staring blankly at their personal comm-pads.)
  Ren (thinking): Same old view. Towering chrome, endless glass. It's beautiful, in a sterile, suffocating way.
  [HUMMMMM (of the monorail)]
  (At the Central Archive, the air is thick with the scent of ozone and recycled air. Rows upon rows of data processing units, each manned by a silent worker.)
  Supervisor Kael: Citizen Ren. On time, as expected. Proceed to Station Gamma-9.
  Ren: Yes, Supervisor.
  (Hours pass. The rhythmic click of data entry, the soft glow of the screen. Ren processes historical records, filtering out anomalies, ensuring narrative consistency.)
  Ren (thinking): Another 'discrepancy' flagged. A minor architectural detail in a pre-Unity era building. Easily corrected. Just a stray pixel, probably.
  [BEEP (of data input)]
  Ren (thinking): Wait.
  (Ren pauses. A flicker on the screen. Not a pixel, but... a shadow. A fleeting, impossible shadow in a historical image of a bustling market street.)
  Ren (thinking): What was that? Did I imagine it?
  [ZAP! (a brief visual distortion on screen)]
  Ren (thinking): There! It happened again! A glitch. But the system didn't flag it.
  (Ren zooms in, trying to pinpoint the anomaly. The image is crisp, clear. No shadow. No distortion.)
  Ren (thinking): Am I tired? Too many cycles without proper rest? No, I felt it. A momentary shift.
  Supervisor Kael: Citizen Ren. Is there an issue with your terminal?
  Ren: No, Supervisor. Just... a momentary lag.
  (Ren forces the thought away, returning to the mundane task. But the unease lingers. A seed of doubt planted in the perfectly ordered garden of Aethelburg.)
  (Later, walking home through the illuminated sky-paths, Ren glances down at the city's lower levels. The industrial sectors, rarely seen, always shrouded in a permanent twilight.)
  Ren (thinking): Just a glitch. That's all it was. The system is flawless.
  (Then, Ren stops dead. Below, in a dark alley between two towering structures, a figure. Not a citizen in a standard uniform. Not a drone. Something... else.)
  [GASP (from Ren)]
  (The figure looks up. Its eyes, glowing with an unnatural light, meet Ren's across the vast chasm of the city. For a split second, Ren sees not a reflection, but a mirror. A parallel.)
  Ren (thinking): Who... what are you?
  [THUMP-THUMP (Ren's heart)]
  (The figure vanishes, swallowed by the shadows. Leaving Ren alone, staring at an empty alley, and a city that suddenly feels very, very wrong.)
