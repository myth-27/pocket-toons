=== Winning Shot Episode 1 ===

  (The worn-out court behind Seoyun High was Ji-Hoon's sanctuary. Every day, after everyone else had gone home, he was here.)
  Kim Ji-Hoon: Just one more.
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (My hands are numb, my legs are burning. But the feeling of the ball... it's everything.)
  [BOUNCE. BOUNCE. BOUNCE.]
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (They call me 'bench warmer' for a reason. Too short, too slow, they say. But they don't see this.)
  [WHOOSH!]
  (He launched a three-pointer from deep, a perfect arc. The ball kissed the rim, hesitated, and then... spun out.)
  [CLANG!]
  Kim Ji-Hoon: Damn it!
  [PANT. PANT.]
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (Another miss. How many has it been today? A hundred? Two hundred? It doesn't matter. I have to make it.)
  (He grabbed the rebound, dribbling instinctively, his eyes scanning an imaginary defense. He wasn't just shooting; he was playing a full game in his head.)
  Kim Ji-Hoon: Screen left... drive baseline... crossover!
  [THWACK! (Ball hitting the ground hard)]
  (He faked a pass, then pulled up sharply, fading away from an invisible defender. It was a shot only a true shooter would attempt.)
  [SWISH!]
  Kim Ji-Hoon: Yes!
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (That feeling... the net swaying, the perfect silence after the shot. It's addicting.)
  Coach Lee: Not bad, Ji-Hoon.
  Kim Ji-Hoon: Coach Lee?!
  (Ji-Hoon froze, his face flushing. He hadn't realized anyone was watching.)
  Coach Lee: You're still here. Every day. I've been watching.
  Kim Ji-Hoon: I... I just like to practice, Coach.
  Coach Lee: You've got a good shot. A real touch. But you lack consistency. And confidence.
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (He knows. He sees it all.)
  Coach Lee: The tryouts for the main team are next week. You're still on the reserve list, right?
  Kim Ji-Hoon: Yes, Coach.
  Coach Lee: I saw you hesitate during the last scrimmage. You had an open look, but you passed. Why?
  Kim Ji-Hoon: I... I thought Min-Joon had a better angle.
  Coach Lee: Min-Joon is a great player, but this isn't about him. It's about you. When you have the shot, you take it. That's what a 'winning shot' is.
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (A winning shot... I've always dreamed of making one. The one that decides the game.)
  Coach Lee: Show me that shot you just made. The fadeaway. Right now. Imagine it's the final second, game on the line.
  (Ji-Hoon felt a surge of adrenaline. This wasn't just practice anymore. This was his chance.)
  [BOUNCE. BOUNCE.]
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (Final second... game on the line... my shot.)
  (He drove hard, planted his foot, and launched himself upwards, leaning back, the ball leaving his fingertips with a prayer.)
  [WHOOSH!]
  [SWISH!]
  (The net rippled. Pure. Undefended. The sound echoed in the twilight.)
  Coach Lee: Good. Now do it when it counts. Next week.
  (Coach Lee turned and walked away, leaving Ji-Hoon standing under the hoop, the ball still bouncing gently. A new fire ignited in his chest.)
  Kim Ji-Hoon (thinking): (Next week. I'll show them. I'll make my winning shot.)
