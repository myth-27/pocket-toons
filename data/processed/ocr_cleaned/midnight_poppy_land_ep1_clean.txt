=== Midnight Poppy Land Episode 1 ===

  (The first hint of dawn barely kissed the sleepy town of Poppy Valley. But inside The Daily Crumb bakery, the day had long begun.)
  POPPY (thinking): Another morning. The smell of yeast and sugar, warm and comforting. It’s a good smell. My smell. My life. Simple, predictable, safe. Just how I like it. No drama, no big surprises. Just flour, eggs, and the occasional burnt pastry.
  [WHIRR]
  [CLATTER]
  [SOFT JINGLE]
  MRS. CHOI: Poppy, dear! Up with the sun, as always. Did you remember to proof the sourdough overnight?
  POPPY: Yes, Mrs. Choi! It's rising beautifully. Almost ready for the oven.
  MRS. CHOI: Good girl. You're a lifesaver, you know that? I swear, without you, this place would crumble faster than a stale croissant.
  POPPY: Oh, you exaggerate! I just like being useful. And I love the smell of fresh bread.
  POPPY (thinking): Mrs. Choi always says that. It makes me feel warm. This bakery... it's been my whole world since I moved here. A quiet haven. Sometimes I wonder if I should want more, but then I look at the perfectly golden loaves, and I think, 'This is enough.'
  [DING!]
  POPPY: Speaking of, the first batch of croissants is done!
  (Later that morning, with the bakery bustling and the aroma of coffee mingling with cinnamon, Poppy took a moment to take out the overflowing trash bins.)
  [SQUEAK]
  [RUMBLE]
  POPPY (thinking): Almost forgot these. The morning rush always leaves a mountain of packaging. At least it's a nice day. Not too hot for August.
  (As she pushed the last bin towards the curb, a delivery truck, emblazoned with a generic 'Logistics Co.' logo, sped a little too close, swerving to avoid a parked car. There was a sudden jolt, and something tumbled from its open back door, landing with a muffled thud near Poppy’s feet.)
  [SCREECH]
  [THUD]
  POPPY: Whoa! Hey!
  (But the truck didn't stop, continuing down the street, oblivious. Poppy stared at the object. It was a rectangular package, wrapped in plain brown paper, surprisingly heavy. No address, no sender. Just... there.)
  POPPY (thinking): What on earth? Did they drop this? It doesn't look like anything for the bakery. No labels... how am I supposed to return it?
  (Curiosity, a rare spark in her usually placid routine, tugged at her. She knelt, picking up the package. It felt cold, almost metallic, beneath the paper. And then she noticed it – a small tear in the wrapping, revealing a glimpse of what lay within.)
  POPPY (thinking): It's... not bread. Or flour. Or anything remotely edible.
  (Through the tear, she saw a sleek, dark object. It looked like a phone, but too smooth, too minimalist, too... expensive for anyone she knew in Poppy Valley. Next to it, a small, black card, almost invisible against the dark interior. On it, a single, stark white symbol – a stylized, geometric poppy.)
  POPPY (thinking): A poppy? Like... Midnight Poppy Land? No, that's just a silly game I play. This is real. This feels... wrong. Dangerous, even. My hands are shaking. What is this? And why did it fall at *my* feet?
  (The warmth of the bakery suddenly felt distant. The comforting scent of bread was replaced by a faint, metallic tang. Poppy clutched the package, her heart beginning to pound a rhythm entirely new to her quiet life. The simple, predictable world she knew had just received a very unexpected, and very unsettling, delivery.)
