=== The Remarried Empress Episode 1 ===

  (The Eastern Empire. A land of prosperity, guided by an Empress whose elegance was only matched by her unwavering resolve. Empress Navier Trovi.)
  NAVIER (thinking): Another dawn. Another day of duties. The sun rises, indifferent to the weight of the crown.
  [RUSTLE]
  COUNTESS ELIZA: Your Majesty, the Imperial Council meeting is scheduled for the third bell. Your attire for the day has been prepared.
  NAVIER: Thank you, Countess. Is the report from the Western Kingdom ready?
  COUNTESS ELIZA: Yes, Your Majesty. It awaits your review.
  NAVIER (thinking): My life has always been a meticulously planned tapestry. Every thread, every color, chosen for the good of the Empire. My marriage to Sovieshu was no different. A political alliance, a duty. Love was a luxury neither of us could afford.
  (Later, in the Emperor's study, the air was thick with the scent of parchment and a familiar chill.)
  NAVIER: Your Majesty, the new trade agreement with the Western Kingdom requires your final approval.
  SOVIESHU: Leave it on the desk, Empress. I will review it later.
  [SCRATCH (of a pen)]
  NAVIER (thinking): Always 'later.' The distance between us grows with each passing day, a chasm of unspoken words and unshared moments. Yet, our roles demand unity, a façade of a perfect imperial couple.
  SOVIESHU: Is there anything else?
  NAVIER: No, Your Majesty. I shall take my leave.
  NAVIER (thinking): The Emperor's attention has been elsewhere. For weeks. I've heard the whispers. A commoner. A slave. Rescued from a hunting accident.
  (The whispers turned to a startling reality during a casual gathering in the palace gardens.)
  SOVIESHU: Everyone, I wish to introduce you to Rashta. She is a delicate flower who has suffered greatly. From this day forward, she will reside in the separate palace.
  [GASPS (from the courtiers)]
  RASHTA: It is an honor... to be here.
  NAVIER (thinking): A commoner. Not even a noble. To be brought into the main palace, given a residence... This is unprecedented. This is not merely a mistress. This is a statement.
  COUNTESS ELIZA: Your Majesty...
  NAVIER (thinking): Maintain composure. An Empress does not falter. Not in public.
  (Later, in the quiet solitude of her private chambers, Navier's thoughts were sharp and clear.)
  NAVIER (thinking): He flaunts her. Openly. Does he seek to provoke me? Or does he genuinely believe this... 'delicate flower' is worthy of such a display? He knows the political implications. He knows the gossip this will incite.
  NAVIER: Countess, arrange for a detailed background check on this 'Rashta.' I want to know everything. Her origins, her past, her connections.
  COUNTESS ELIZA: At once, Your Majesty. Are you... alright?
  NAVIER: I am the Empress, Countess. I am always alright.
  NAVIER (thinking): But the foundations of my life, the very structure of my marriage, are shifting. He is not just taking a mistress; he is bringing chaos into the imperial household. And I, Navier Trovi, will not stand by and watch the Empire destabilize.
  (From a discreet distance, Navier observed Rashta, who was laughing and playing with a small bird in the imperial gardens.)
  [CHIRP]
  NAVIER (thinking): She is... innocent, perhaps. Or merely skilled at feigning it. Either way, she is a pawn in a game she may not even understand. But a pawn can still cause immense damage.
  (The Empress, known for her unyielding resolve, felt a chill settle in her heart. The first crack had appeared in the gilded cage of her imperial life. And with it, the seeds of a new destiny began to sprout.)
